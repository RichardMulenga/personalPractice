Course Introduction

Computer Hardware Design and Implementation introduces students to the fundamental principles involved in the design, structure, and operation of modern computer hardware systems. The course focuses on how computing devices are built at the hardware level and how different components interact to execute programs and process data. The course begins with the basic architecture of microprocessors and microcontrollers, explaining how the Central Processing Unit (CPU) performs instruction processing through the fetch–decode–execute cycle. Students are introduced to the internal organization of processors, including registers, arithmetic logic units (ALU), control units, buses, and memory interfaces. Building on these foundations, the course explores widely used processor families such as the Intel 8085, Intel 8086, and microcontroller architectures like the 8051 Microcontroller, providing insight into their instruction sets, addressing modes, and hardware organization. Emphasis is placed on understanding how these components are designed and implemented to perform efficient computation. The course also introduces the role of microcontrollers in embedded systems, highlighting how hardware and software interact to control real-world devices. Students learn how hardware resources such as timers, input/output ports, interrupts, and memory modules are integrated into microcontroller-based systems. By the end of the course, students will have a solid understanding of computer hardware structure and will be able to analyze processor architectures, understand hardware-level program execution, and appreciate the principles behind the design and implementation of digital computing systems.
