Course Introduction

Multimedia and Computer Human Interaction explores the technologies and design principles that enable effective interaction between humans and computer systems through multimedia content. The course introduces the fundamental concepts of multimedia systems, including the representation, processing, storage, and transmission of different media types such as text, images, graphics, audio, and video. Students learn how multimedia data is digitally encoded and compressed to enable efficient storage and communication. The course also examines multimedia networking, streaming technologies, and the integration of various media forms into interactive applications. A major focus of the course is human–computer interaction (HCI), which studies how users interact with computer systems and how interfaces can be designed to improve usability, accessibility, and user experience. Topics include interface design principles, user-centered design, usability evaluation, and interactive multimedia applications. By the end of the course, students will understand how multimedia technologies and HCI principles combine to create engaging, efficient, and user-friendly digital systems used in areas such as education, entertainment, communication, and web applications.
